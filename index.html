We've deployed a new Python-based risk model for portfolio analysis.


import numpy as np

def calculate_portfolio_volatility(weights, returns_df):
    # Calculate daily returns covariance matrix
    cov_matrix = returns_df.cov() * 252 # Annualize
    
    # Calculate portfolio variance
    port_variance = np.dot(weights.T, np.dot(cov_matrix, weights))
    
    # Calculate portfolio volatility (standard deviation)
    port_volatility = np.sqrt(port_variance)
    return port_volatility

Financial Modeling & Projections

Our project valuation models are based on standard financial principles.

Net Present Value (NPV)

We use NPV to evaluate the profitability of new ventures:

$$ NPV = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{CF_t}{(1 + r)^t} - C_0 $$

Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)

To measure long-term performance:

$$ CAGR = \left( \frac{\text{Ending Value}}{\text{Beginning Value}} \right)^{\frac{1}{n}} - 1 $$
Looking forward, our capital allocation strategy is disciplined and data-driven. Our primary tool for evaluating new projects is the Net Present Value formula, which allows us to discount future cash flows back to today's value. (Click 1) We also use CAGR extensively to smooth out volatility and understand the true annual growth rate of our key metrics over time. These tools ensure we invest in projects that create real, quantifiable value.
